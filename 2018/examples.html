Over a yearlong partnership with the MAISD Regional Mathematics and Science Center [SHOULD THIS BE A LINK SOMEWHERE?], researchers helped teachers collect and code 2000 student drawings from 10 Western Michigan elementary schools. Participating classrooms and schools voluntarily participated in the math drawing exercise, so these results are not intended to inform inferences to other school settings or populations. Indeed, the drawings shown here were initially collected and coded to spur reflection with individual teachers around teaching math. However, through the use of a quantitative coding rubric, large numbers of drawings can be analyzed for trends and patterns within and across school communities.
